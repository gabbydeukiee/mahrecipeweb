Spaghetti

Spaghetti is a long, thin, cylindrical pasta that originated in Italy and is commonly made from durum wheat semolina. It’s often served with a variety of sauces, such as marinara, Bolognese, or carbonara, making it a staple in many cuisines around the world..

Philippine Adobo

Philippine adobo is a popular Filipino dish made by simmering meat—commonly chicken or pork—in a savory and tangy mixture of soy sauce, vinegar, garlic, bay leaves, and black pepper. It's known for its rich, flavorful taste and is often served with steamed rice.
